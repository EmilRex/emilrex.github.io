Projects

Searching for Water on Exo-Planets (2014)

The summer after I finished my undergraduate studies I had the good fortune of working with Christine Chen at the Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI) in Baltimore.At STScI I worked on a model of infrared spectral energy distributions (SEDs) with silicate and water-ice features. The ultimate purpose of the model was to examine where and when ice appears in star systems. The code is available on GitHub and you can see my final presentation here (slides).

Hunting for Galaxies Far, Far Away (2012-2014)

At James Madison University, where I studied Physics, I worked with Anca Constantin on an extensive classification study of masers. A maser is a galaxy which exhibits a specific type of strong radio emission. Masers are important in astrophysics because they establish highly accurate and independent distance measurements to far away objects. However, there are not many known masers and discovering them is an expensive and laborious process. So this project aimed to increase the probability of discovery by correlating other characteristics of maser hosting galaxies with the occurence of masers. This project also where I learned the fundementals of coding as well as a bit of machine learning in the form of PCA. I even managed to pick up a bit of Python.

I presented my research at the 11th CAA Undergraduate Research Conference at Old Dominion University in April 2013 and at the 223rd meeting of the American Astronomical Society in Washington, DC in January 2014 (abstract). I also presented my research at the 2014 JMU Physics and Astronomy Symposium where my presentation won 1st place. Dr. Constantin and I were even lucky enough to be featured in JMU's Madison Magazine.
